When to Walk Away from the Battle

By Nithish Chandra · Mahabharata · Modern Life

Arjuna stood still on the battlefield of Kurukshetra—not because he lacked strength, but because clarity had abandoned him.

This moment is not ancient history. It repeats itself every day in modern offices, relationships, and moral crossroads—when the cost of winning feels heavier than the pain of walking away.

Sometimes dharma does not demand victory.
It demands restraint.

We inherit battles we did not choose—expectations, careers, identities. Like Arjuna, we are often told to fight because tradition demands it, not because the fight still matters.

To walk away is mistaken for weakness. In truth, it is discipline. Krishna did not command blind warfare—he offered perspective.
