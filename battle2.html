What We Refuse to Let Go Of

By Nithish Chandra · Mahabharata · Modern Life

Karna stood beside Duryodhana—not because he was blind to injustice, but because loyalty had become his identity.

He knew the path was wrong.
He knew the end was near.
Yet he stayed.

Not out of weakness, but out of attachment.

This is not ancient history. It repeats itself every day—in careers, relationships, and beliefs—when letting go feels more frightening than staying trapped.

Sometimes strength is not holding on.
It is knowing when to release.

We carry things long after they stop serving us—old labels, fading bonds, outdated dreams. Like Karna, we confuse endurance with virtue.

This is who I am.

This is my duty.

This is my fate.

So we remain loyal to what slowly drains us.

To walk away is seen as betrayal.
In truth, it is self-respect.

Karna was noble.
But nobility without awareness becomes sacrifice without purpose.

Letting go does not erase your past.
It frees your future.

Minimalism is not about having less.
It is about needing less.

Less approval.

Less fear.

Less emotional baggage.

When you release what no longer belongs, you create space for clarity.

And clarity is freedom.
